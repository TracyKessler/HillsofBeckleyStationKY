Annual Assessment

Annual HOA dues are also collected the first quarter of each year. An invoice will be sent to each resident with the exact ammount due, the address to submit payment, and the date the payment is due.

The Community Association Annual Meeting takes place in March. The meetings are held at the Middletown Government Center, located on Juneau Drive, behind the McDonalds. A flyer will be placed in everyone's mailbox with specific deatils to the day and time of the meeting. Attendance is greatly appreciated. It is the best venue for voicing concerns, obtaining information about the state of the community assoication, and helping to maintain our goal of making this community a great place to live.

Snow Removal

During the event of snow, Hills of Beckley Stations has a contract with a local company to salt and scrape the neighborhood streets. The HBS does its best to ensure that our streets are kept safe for busses and commuters. Please do your part by clearing the sidewalks infront of your home for our community pedestrians.

Municipal Agencies

Use these links when setting up your utilities or to report any community issues. To report issues with street lights, power outages, or downed lines call 502-589-1444. Before digging to install deck, planting new trees, or adding an addition to your home call 811. To report a gas leak call 502-589-5511. To report a concern with street signs, animals, garbage, parks, or property maintenance call 311.

Local Amenities

Beckly Creek is just a minutes drive from The Hills of Beckley Station, making it the closest section of The Parklands. Features of the park include the 22-acre Egg Lawn, the PNC Achievement Center for Education, The Marshal Playground and Splashground, and a variety of trails.

Just under 4 miles away, the Middletown Library is the closest branch to The Hills of Beckley Station. The Middletown Library serves residents from a municipal building also housing the Louisville Metro Police’s 8th Division, a County Clerk’s office, Driver’s Licensing bureau, Vocational Rehabilitation, and a Department of Health office.

The House of Boom Extreme Air Sports, is a buliding full of trampolines and great fun. Located near the heart of Middletown, The House of Boom includes basketball hoops, chains to swing on, foam pits, and even a climbing wall

Valhalla Golf Club is known as a modern paradise for championship golf. It was designed by all-time major Champion Jack Nicklaus. Valhalla is ranked No. 1 in Kentucky and among “America’s 100 Greatest Courses” by Golf Digest. Valhalla has hosted three PGA Championships, two Senior PGA Championships, and of course the 2008 Ryder Cup.
